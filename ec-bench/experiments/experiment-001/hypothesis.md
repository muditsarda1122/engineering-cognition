# Research Hypotheses

EC-Bench evaluates whether accumulated Engineering Cognition changes the quality of long-horizon software engineering.

The benchmark compares two identical engineering environments.

The **only independent variable** is the availability of accumulated Engineering Cognition.

---

# Null Hypothesis (H0)

Accumulated Engineering Cognition produces **no statistically significant improvement** in software engineering performance over stateless reasoning.

Any observed differences are attributable to chance, model variance, prompt ordering, or unrelated factors rather than accumulated engineering understanding.

---

# Primary Hypothesis (H1)

Accumulated Engineering Cognition improves **Architectural Continuity** across long-running software engineering workflows.

---

# Secondary Hypothesis (H2)

Accumulated Engineering Cognition increases **Repository Groundedness**, allowing the agent to engineer the current repository rather than repeatedly reasoning from generic software knowledge.

---

# Secondary Hypothesis (H3)

Accumulated Engineering Cognition increases **Engineering Cognition Reuse**, reducing repeated reasoning and enabling engineering understanding to compound across sessions.

---

# Secondary Hypothesis (H4)

Accumulated Engineering Cognition improves overall **Engineering Quality**, producing stronger engineering decisions over time.

---

# Secondary Hypothesis (H5)

Accumulated Engineering Cognition improves **Debugging and Investigation Efficiency**, allowing agents to investigate problems more systematically as engineering understanding accumulates.

---

# Research Question

Can accumulated Engineering Cognition measurably improve long-horizon software engineering beyond what stateless reasoning alone can achieve?

If so,

- which engineering behaviors improve first,
- how quickly does improvement emerge,
- and what extraction strategies maximize those improvements?
